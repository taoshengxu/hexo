L1000 mRNA profiling assay

L1000 is a gene-expression profiling assay based on the direct measurement of a reduced representation of the transcriptome and computational inference of the portion of the transcriptome not explicitly measured. The number of landmark transcripts whose abundance is measured directly is approximately one thousand. Eighty additional invariant transcripts are also explicitly measured to enable quality control, scaling and normalization. Measurements of transcript abundance are made with a combination of a coupled ligase detection and polymerase chain reaction, optically-addressed microspheres, and a flow-cytometric detection system. The dataset consists of a collection of transcriptional expression data from cultured human cells treated with bioactive small molecules and genetic perturbagens profiled on the Broad Institute L1000 assay. Each well contains a biological sample and fluorescent Luminex beads. When a plate is scanned, the Luminex scanner reads off two values from each bead in each well. The color identifies the two possible genes that the bead may be measuring, and the intensity identifies the expression of the gene being measured. The two genes for each bead color are chosen such that one is a high expresser, and one is a low expresser. The scanning process yields intensity data for each bead in each well. Consider all beads of a particular color in a particular well. If there are 100 such beads, then roughly 70 of them detect the presence of some gene a, while roughly 30 detect the presence of gene b. Because we only have color and intensity information on each bead, we cannot measure directly which of the two genes a particular bead detects. However, we can indirectly determine the expression levels of genes a and b. To deconvolute the expression levels, a histogram where the x axis is the fluorescence intensity, and the y axis is the number of beads measured to fluoresce at that intensity is constructed. As most of the beads detect the presence of gene a, the larger peak indicates the expression of this gene. Similarly the smaller peak indicates the expression of gene b. The peaks are distinct because the genes were chosen to express at different levels. The peaks are separated using the K-means clustering algorithm. These values are further normalized to account for differences in overall fluorescence measurements between different plates, beads, and scanners. There are two sequential normalization steps: Luminex Invariant Set Normalization (LISS), and Quantile Normalization (QNORM). Normalization yields the expression values of the 978 Landmark L1000 genes. To obtain expression values for all genes in the transcriptome, it is assumed that an unmeasured gene can be predicted from the measured landmark genes via linear regression. To obtain a measure of relative gene expression, a Z-scoring procedure is used to generate signatures from normalized profiles. In the case of L1000 data, all signatures are represented by vectors measuring differential expression. For gene knockdown data, a single treatment represents a hairpin targeting a single gene. Because these hairpins are known to have off-target effects, the effects of targeting the actual gene are summarized over the different hairpins into concensus gene score (CGS).
